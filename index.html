To specify therefore enough oil to cover the bottom of your saucepan or about a teacup of olive oil is a short cut. It is also an indication that a precise quantity is not of great moment. Except for sauces, one does not often measure oil by tablespoons. One pours it out of a bottle into the pan One uses one's eye and one's loaf.”
